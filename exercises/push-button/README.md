### Pushbuttons (Tactile Switches)

In this folder, we are going to explore **pushbuttons**. Virtually every electronic device features at least one button, even if it is just to power it on. At its core, a button operates within two distinct states: **ON** and **OFF**.
 

### Characteristics:
The pushbuttons used in these exercises feature **4 terminals** that are internally connected in pairs. 
They provide a practical and direct way for users to send commands to the microcontroller, whether to activate an LED, trigger a process, navigate a menu, or toggle a device's state.

### How They Work:
* **Momentary Action:** They are momentary devices, meaning they only remain active while they are actively being pressed.
* **Automatic Return:** They automatically spring back to their original, open state as soon as they are released.
* **Pulse Generation:** They generate a temporary electrical pulse (`HIGH` or `LOW`) upon being pressed, depending on your circuit's pull-up or pull-down configuration.
