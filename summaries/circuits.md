### What is a Circuit?

A circuit is an interconnection of electronic components that provides a closed path for electric current to flow. These components can include:

* **Diodes:** Allow current to flow in only one direction.
* **Resistors:** Resist and limit the flow of electrical current.
* **Capacitors:** Store electrical energy.
* **Inductors:** Store energy in a magnetic field.
* **Transistors:** Amplify or switch electrical signals and power.
* **Power Sources & Switches:** Voltage/current sources (to provide power) and switches (to control the flow).

The behavior of a circuit depends heavily on how these components are connected. They can be wired in **series** or in **parallel** with each other, which fundamentally changes how the electrical energy is distributed across the system.

Electric current always seeks the path of least resistance. We use this principle to intentionally control and distribute energy exactly how we want it. We can calculate and predict this flow of energy using **Ohm's Law**.
