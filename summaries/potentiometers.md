### What is a Potentiometer?
It is a type of variable resistor that allows you to adjust the voltage in a circuit. Potentiometers are commonly used for:
* Calibration circuits
* Precision voltage control
* Radio volume control
* Brightness control (dimmers)

### Characteristics:
It consists of a resistor with 3 terminals: one **center terminal** (wiper) and two **outer terminals**.

### How it Works:
Moving the wiper (cursor) changes the resistance between the center terminal and the outer terminals. 

When connected to 5V and GND, the potentiometer acts as a voltage divider: moving the wiper closer to GND drops the output voltage toward 0V, while moving it closer to the 5V source raises it toward 5V.

* **High Impedance:** Allows the signal to reach 5V freely.
* **Low Impedance:** Drains the signal straight to GND (stuck at 0V).
