### What is a Resistor?
A resistor is an electrical component designed to oppose the flow of electrical current in a circuit. This opposition consequently transforms electrical energy into thermal energy (heat) through the Joule effect. This opposition to the current is called resistance.

A voltage drop also occurs across the resistor due to the potential difference created as the current passes through it.

> **Example:** If the voltage before the resistor is 10V and after it is 2V, it means a voltage drop of 8V occurred.

### Characteristics:
* **Fixed Resistors:** Their resistance value cannot be altered. Common types include carbon film, metal film, and wirewound resistors.
* **Variable Resistors:** Their resistance can be adjusted manually (e.g., a potentiometer).
* **Unit of Measurement:** The unit of resistance is the Ohm (represented by the Greek letter Ω).

### To remember:
* The higher the resistance, the lower the current flow.

> **Tip:** Want to know the resistance value of a specific resistor? Read its color bands! Standard resistors use a color code system to indicate their value and tolerance.
